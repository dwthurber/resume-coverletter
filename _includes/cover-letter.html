Cover Letter Workshop

Tailor a cover letter to the job

Cover letters are a way to stand out from the pile. Hiring managers expect to see specific experience in your cover letter related to the job description. For example, you might want to start by going through a job description and looking for the key areas you can highlight (literally highlighting can help) in your cover letter. 

Start by looking at the following job description for a fictional higher education job.

Sample Cover Letter

Now look at how the following sample cover letter is tailored to that job description. Notice how the highlighting matches to the highlighted sections of the job description.

Arisleda Johnson

1234 Chicago Ave., Apt. 1B, Evanston, IL 60201 
ariseldajohnson2016@u.northwestern.edu | 987-654-3210 | https://linkedin.com/in/AriseldaJohnson


 

January 15, 2016

 

Ms. Toni Riley
Director of Undergraduate Admission
Illinois Institute of Technology
Office of Undergraduate Admission
10 West 33rd Street
Perlstein Hall, Room 101
Chicago, IL 60616
 

Dear Ms. Riley:

Your “This is Illinois Tech” YouTube video is a powerful recruitment tool. Between that video, your impressive viewbook, the Fast Forward program, and the finance tools provided on your website, it is clear you have a lot to offer potential Illinois Tech applicants. I would be honored and excited to use these resources as your new Senior Admissions Counselor, committed to recruiting innovative and entrepreneurial thinkers to your programs.

Your job description states that you are seeking an excellent communicator with extraordinary customer service skills. As an admissions graduate assistant for Northwestern University and in my previous positions, I have demonstrated an ability to connect well with individuals and large groups alike. My presentations, written materials, and strategic programming have contributed to a 200% increase in mentor applications at DePaul and 11% increase in membership growth at Rotary International. Additionally, my team spirit and relationship-building skills have led my superiors and colleagues to select me for promotions and special projects. This track record for success will help me to represent and market Illinois Tech effectively and to support the enrollment goals of your institution.

As I counsel your prospective students and their families on the admission process, I will use technology, as well as critical thinking and analytical skills, to my advantage. Having learned Northwestern’s recruitment software very quickly, I’m confident that I can do the same with yours, updating your databases and running reports that will aid admissions decisions and improve operations, too. Not only have I demonstrated my abilities in this area through evaluation of up to 75 Northwestern applications per week, I honed my research skills through my recent master’s project, too. My findings about a student’s first-year experience at an institution will inform and improve my ability to assess “fit” between an applicant and Illinois Tech.

While I’ve tried to address the requirements stated in the Senior Admissions Counselor job description, I know there is more to learn. Will you please grant me an interview so that we can talk in detail about your needs for this position and how I can exceed your expectations? Thank you very much for your consideration of my application. I look forward to hearing from you!
 

Sincerely,

 

Ariselda Johnson
